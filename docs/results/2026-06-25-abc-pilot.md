# ABC fast-cycle GPU pilot — Variant A vs Variant B (T10)

**Date:** 2026-06-25  **Branch:** worktree off `feat/halludesign-chai-dpeptide`
(tip `b9cda3b`)
**Runtime:** chai_lab 0.6.1, image `gradio_design-gradio-design:latest`, 2× RTX A4500, ESM cache
`/home/user/chai_weights_cache`. Driver: `scripts/design.py --search abc`.
**Case:** free mixed-chirality cyclic peptide, **no target** (`--binder_class cyclic
--target_type none`), `--binder_length 12` (EPpKPp scale). **Closure restraint only** (head-to-tail
COVALENT, on chain A) — NO Zn / NO coordination terms. Objective = `0.7*pTM + 0.3*termini_proximity`
at K*=15 fast diffusion steps (the 2026-06-25 cyclization-calibration decision).
**Load split:** Variant A on **GPU 0**, Variant B on **GPU 1**, run **in parallel**.

## Question

Does the fast-cycle ABC search actually optimize the pTM+termini objective end-to-end on GPU, and
which variant converges better **per GPU-hour**? (A = ABC searches chirality, MPNN fills identity;
B = ABC searches identity+chirality, MPNN warm-start only.)

## Wiring fixes found and applied (the pilot would not measure the objective without these)

1. **`Prediction._cif_path` was never populated** (`xenodesign/backends/chai_backend.py`). The ABC
   fitness reads `pred._cif_path` to compute the C–N termini distance; the real `ChaiBackend.predict`
   returned a `Prediction` with no such attribute, so `getattr(pred,"_cif_path",None)` was always
   `None` and the fitness silently collapsed to **`0.7*pTM` only** — the 0.3 termini term (the whole
   point of the cyclization objective) was dead on the GPU path. Fix: add the `_cif_path` field and
   set it to the selected model's CIF in `load_prediction`. (The fitness unit test's fake backend
   already set `_cif_path`; the real path just never matched that contract.)

2. **Variant A double-encoded the identity → permanent `-inf`** (`xenodesign/abc/variants.py`).
   `abc_variant_a_design_fn` returned `result.d_fasta` (a parenthesized `(DXX)` mixed-chirality
   FASTA), but the engine carries that string as the *plain* `identity` and the fitness re-applies
   `mixed_chirality_fasta(identity, …)` to it → `KeyError "unknown amino-acid letter '('"` → the
   fitness `except` returns `-inf`. **Every** MPNN-designed candidate evaluated to `-inf`, so Variant
   A could never keep a move and was frozen on the warm-start seed (the first, broken A pilot left
   only **2** completed eval dirs and never improved). Fix: return `result.one_letter` (the plain
   identity); the fitness adapter owns the per-position handedness emit. Added regression test
   `test_variant_a_returns_plain_identity_not_dfasta` (asserts no parentheses + round-trips through
   `mixed_chirality_fasta`).

3. **ABC convergence history was not persisted** (`xenodesign/dispatch.py`). `_run_abc` captured
   `abc_search`'s per-cycle `history` but only saved `n_cycles=len(history)`. Added `"history"` to
   `abc_result.json` so the convergence curve is recoverable for future runs. (For this pilot the
   curves below were reconstructed post-hoc from the per-eval chai outputs via `abc_runs/analyze.py`,
   which recomputes the identical nectar and **matches the engine's `selected_nectar` exactly**.)

A host-only docker fix was also needed: the repo's `LigandMPNN/model_params` symlink points to an
absolute host path outside the `$PWD:/work` mount, so Variant A's MPNN weights weren't visible in the
container. Added a read-only bind of that path to the same path inside the container. (No code change.)

CPU suite stays green throughout: **860 passed, 30 deselected**; all 59 ABC tests pass.

## Convergence (best nectar vs eval)

**Variant A (fixed)** — explores diversely but **never beats the warm-start seed**:

| eval | 1 | 4 | 13 | 18 | 26 | 29 | 30 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| nectar | **0.735** | 0.542 | 0.688 | 0.724 | 0.724 | 0.725 | 0.613 |
| running-best | 0.735 | 0.735 | 0.735 | 0.735 | 0.735 | 0.735 | 0.735 |

Best = **eval 1 (the seed)**: pTM 0.62, C–N termini **1.30 Å (closed ring)**, nectar **0.7346**. The
30 MPNN-designed candidates reach pTM 0.4–0.64 with many near-closed termini (cn < 2 Å — the closure
restraint works), but none beat the curated alternating-L/D seed. **Root cause:** Variant A's MPNN
designs on a **zero backbone** (`np.zeros((n,4,3))` — the T9 "inject the last predicted structure's
backbone" step is still unwired), so the identity oracle is structure-blind and cannot produce a
coherent high-pTM fold. The search *machinery* is now correct (post-fix it genuinely varies identity
+ chirality every move); the *identity oracle* is starved.

**Variant B** — genuinely **converges** (running-best improves **7 times** over the 90-eval run):

| eval (improvement) | 1 | 6 | 14 | 19 | 30 | 58 | 80 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| running-best | 0.718 | 0.742 | 0.766 | 0.789 | 0.807 | 0.820 | **0.841** |

A clean monotone ABC climb from the seed (0.718) to **0.841** — the colony exploits high-nectar
sources and keeps finding better ones late (last improvement at eval 80). The objective is doing real
work: late high-pTM-but-open candidates (e.g. pTM 0.89 at cn 18 Å → nectar 0.64) are correctly ranked
*below* the moderate-pTM closed winner.

## Best designs

- **Variant A:** `MQALEHQQQIGH`, chirality `LDLDLDLDLDLD` → chai emit
  `M(DGN)A(DLE)E(DHI)Q(DGN)Q(DIL)G(DHI)`. nectar **0.7346** (pTM 0.62, cn **1.30 Å**, prox 1.00). A
  plausible *closed* alternating-L/D macrocycle — but it is just the seed, not a search win.
- **Variant B:** `MGVRIFQQFGAP`, chirality `LLDDDDDDLDDD` → chai emit
  `MG(DVA)(DAR)(DIL)(DPN)(DGN)(DGN)FG(DAL)(DPR)`. nectar **0.8414** (pTM **0.773**, cn **1.21 Å**,
  prox 1.00), found at eval 80. A genuinely **high-pTM, closed** mixed-chirality 12-mer macrocycle
  (2 L-prefix + mostly-D body) — a real search win over the seed and a plausible cyclizable design.

## VERDICT

- **Does fast-cycle ABC optimize the objective end-to-end on GPU?** **Yes — once the two fitness/
  encoding bugs are fixed.** With `_cif_path` wired, the termini term is live and discriminating:
  high-pTM-but-open designs (pTM ~0.87, cn 15–20 Å) are correctly scored *below* moderate-pTM closed
  rings — exactly the cyclization signal the objective is meant to reward. Variant B's running-best
  climbs monotonically (0.718 → 0.841), proving the colony exploits and improves.
- **Which variant converges better per GPU-hour?** **Variant B, decisively.** B reached **0.841** in
  41.0 min (90 evals, ~27 s/eval → ~132 evals/GPU-h, +0.124 nectar over the seed); A stalled at the
  seed (**0.735**) and is ~3× costlier per eval (~34 s/eval incl. MPNN). Per GPU-hour B is the **only**
  variant that moves the objective at all.
- **Is it ready to scale?** **B: yes** — scale colony/cycles and lengths now. **A: not until the T9
  backbone injection is wired** — without a real per-move backbone, MPNN is structure-blind and A
  cannot beat a hand-built seed. Recommend: ship B as the default ABC arm; defer A behind T9.
